Photo-Based
Ad Campaign

Three campaign ideas. Each one built around a moment your audience already knows.

April 2026
The Approach
Recognition is faster
than persuasion.
Photo-based creative puts Life Dash inside someone's life before they've agreed to care about it. The person scrolling doesn't have to imagine anything — they see a kitchen counter that looks like their kitchen counter. A waiting room they've sat in. A piece of paper they've held too many times.

Every execution in this campaign is built around a moment the audience already knows. We're not explaining what Life Dash does. We're showing someone who already has it.
Idea 01

Medication & Lab Results

The broadest idea. Anyone managing long-term medication who has never been able to see whether it's working.

01
Idea 01 · Path A
See If It's Working
No person. No demographic. No condition. Just the question that anyone on any long-term medication has had in the back of their mind for years — and a data visual that shows the answer exists.

Split-frame still: paper on one side, a graph on the other. The simplest execution in the set and the broadest possible audience.
Ad Headline
"See If It's Working."
Idea 01 · Path B
Your Pills. Your Results. Connected.
We're in the kitchen — morning pills, coffee, phone on the counter. The shift here is from anxiety to agency. We're not telling someone they have a problem. We're showing someone who already has their life sorted and Life Dash is the piece that makes it complete.

Flat-lay format. Native to Instagram Stories and Facebook Feed — reads as content, not an ad.
Ad Headline
"Your Pills. Your Results. Connected."
Idea 01 · Path C
Six Portals. One Dashboard. Free.
Before-and-after diptych — genuine chaos on the left, clean dashboard on the right. Anyone who has tried to manage their health across multiple providers will count to six in their head before they finish reading the headline.

That moment of recognition is the click. The strongest retargeting piece in the set.
Ad Headline
"Six Portals. One Dashboard. Free."
Idea 02

The GLP-1 Generation

The most defined audience in the set. High intent, medically engaged, and almost completely underserved by existing tools.

02
Idea 02 · Path A
Beyond The Scale
The whole market is obsessing over one number. Life Dash is the only product asking about everything else.

Week one versus week twenty-four. Sparse versus rich. We're not positioning against weight loss — we're expanding what success looks like. That's a completely open space right now.
Ad Headline
"Beyond The Scale."
Idea 02 · Path B
Walk In Already Knowing
A patient showing a doctor their own data. Shot from behind the patient — they're the one leading the conversation.

Every person with a long-term condition has sat in that room not knowing what they're about to be told. This ad shows them the other version of that moment. We're not selling a product feature — we're selling a feeling most patients have never had.
Ad Headline
"Walk In Already Knowing."
Idea 02 · Path C
What Is GLP-1 Doing?
A close-up of a multi-line graph — four markers tracked together. One dips, the next follows.

The headline is the question people are already Googling. We don't answer it in the ad — the graph implies the answer exists inside the product. That gap is the click. Pairs directly with Google search intent keywords.
Ad Headline
"What Is GLP-1 Doing?"
Idea 03

Cholesterol Treadmill

Targets a frustration nobody is currently advertising to. Millions of people take a statin every morning and have no idea if it's doing anything.

03
Idea 03 · Path A
Three Years. One Graph.
A man at his kitchen table. Phone showing a two-year graph. Family photo blurred in the background.

The emotional payoff isn't fear — it's proof of effort. He did the unglamorous work. He took the pill every day. Life Dash is the first thing that's ever shown him it worked. That's a profound moment and it's made quiet — which is exactly right.
Ad Headline
"Three Years. One Graph."
Idea 03 · Path B
18 Months Of Truth
A lab printout with a single number highlighted: 187. Next to a Life Dash graph showing where that number went over 18 months.

That piece of paper you held in the car park outside the doctor's office was not the whole story. The whole story is what happened after — and for the first time, you can see it. The 18-month figure feels like a real person's experience, not a round marketing number.
Ad Headline
"18 Months Of Truth."
Idea 03 · Path C
No Waiting To Know
The waiting room flipped. The anxiety is gone. She's sitting there comparing her new lab results to a trend she's been watching for a year. The doctor walks in and she's already nodding.

Quiet confidence. Not urgency. That's a tone most health advertising never reaches — and the double meaning in the headline earns its scroll stop.
Ad Headline
"No Waiting To Know."
